📊 N5 Test Overview

The JLPT N5 test has 3 sections: Language Knowledge (vocabulary/kanji), Reading, and Listening. The test lasts about 105 minutes total. You need ~80/180 points to pass (no individual section cutoff for N5).

🗺️ Recommended Learning Order

1. Learn Hiragana completely (1–2 weeks) → 2. Learn Katakana (1–2 weeks) → 3. Basic vocabulary + phrases → 4. Grammar patterns → 5. Kanji (learn alongside vocab) → 6. Reading & Listening practice → 7. Mock tests

🔤 The Japanese Writing Systems

Hiragana (ひらがな)
46 characters. Used for native Japanese words, verb endings, particles. Round, curvy shapes. Learn this FIRST.
Katakana (カタカナ)
46 characters. Used for foreign loanwords, scientific terms, emphasis. Angular, straight shapes. Learn this SECOND.
Kanji (漢字)
Chinese-origin characters. N5 requires 103 kanji. Each has ON reading (Chinese origin) and KUN reading (Japanese). Learn gradually alongside vocabulary.
Romaji (ローマ字)
Latin alphabet representation of Japanese sounds. Useful for beginners but avoid relying on it — learn the native scripts!

📝 Essential N5 Grammar Patterns

🧱 Core Sentence Structure

Subject は Predicate。
は (wa) is the topic marker. Japanese is Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) order.
わたしは がくせい です。
Watashi wa gakusei desu.
I am a student.

⚓ Particles (助詞)

は (wa) — Topic Marker
Marks the topic of the sentence. Pronounced "wa" not "ha".
田中さんは先生です。
Tanaka-san wa sensei desu.
Mr. Tanaka is a teacher.
が (ga) — Subject Marker
Marks the grammatical subject, especially for new information or emphasis.
だれが きましたか？
Dare ga kimashita ka?
Who came?
を (wo/o) — Object Marker
Marks the direct object of the verb. Pronounced "o".
りんごを たべます。
Ringo o tabemasu.
I eat an apple.
に (ni) — Location / Time / Direction
Marks location of existence, destination, or specific time.
３じに がっこうに いきます。
San-ji ni gakkou ni ikimasu.
I go to school at 3 o'clock.
で (de) — Location of Action / Means
Marks where an action takes place, or the means/tool used.
こうえんで あそびます。
Kouen de asobimasu.
I play in the park.
の (no) — Possessive / Connection
Links nouns. Like 's in English or "of".
わたしの ほん。
Watashi no hon.
My book.
と (to) — And (nouns) / With
Connects nouns ("A and B") or means "together with".
ねことイヌ。 / ともだちとえいがをみます。
Neko to inu. / Tomodachi to eiga o mimasu.
Cat and dog. / I watch a movie with a friend.
も (mo) — Also / Too
Replaces は or が to mean "also" or "too".
わたしも がくせいです。
Watashi mo gakusei desu.
I am also a student.
か (ka) — Question Marker
Added to end of sentence to form a question (no rising intonation needed in formal).
にほんごが わかりますか？
Nihongo ga wakarimasu ka?
Do you understand Japanese?
ね (ne) / よ (yo) — Sentence-Final Particles
ね = seeking agreement ("right?"); よ = asserting information ("you know!")
いいてんきですね。 / これはおいしいですよ！
Ii tenki desu ne. / Kore wa oishii desu yo!
Nice weather, isn't it? / This is delicious!

🔄 Verb Conjugation

～ます / ～ません (masu / masen) — Present/Future
Polite present or future. ～ます = affirmative, ～ません = negative.
たべます / たべません
tabemasu / tabemasen
I eat / I don't eat
～ました / ～ませんでした — Past Tense
Polite past tense (affirmative and negative).
きのう えいがをみました。
Kinou eiga o mimashita.
I watched a movie yesterday.
～て います (te imasu) — Ongoing Action
Indicates an action in progress (like English "-ing").
いま べんきょうして います。
Ima benkyou shite imasu.
I am studying right now.
～たいです (tai desu) — Want to do
Express desire to do something. Replace ます with たい.
にほんに いきたいです。
Nihon ni ikitai desu.
I want to go to Japan.

🏗️ Key Sentence Patterns

N は N です。— A is B
Basic identity / description sentence.
これは ほんです。
Kore wa hon desu.
This is a book.
N は Adj です。— A is [adjective]
For い-adj: just add です. For な-adj: add な before noun, or です at end.
このほんは おもしろいです。 / あのひとは しずかです。
Kono hon wa omoshiroi desu. / Ano hito wa shizuka desu.
This book is interesting. / That person is quiet.
N1 は N2 に あります / います。— X is in/at Y
あります for inanimate objects, います for people/animals.
ねこは へやに います。/ ほんは つくえの うえに あります。
Neko wa heya ni imasu. / Hon wa tsukue no ue ni arimasu.
The cat is in the room. / The book is on the desk.
～から ～まで — From ~ to ~
Indicates range of time or place.
９じから５じまで はたらきます。
Ku-ji kara go-ji made hatarakimasu.
I work from 9 to 5.
～てから — After doing ~
Action B happens after action A.
ごはんを たべてから、はをみがきます。
Gohan o tabete kara, ha o migakimasu.
After eating, I brush my teeth.
〜ないでください — Please don't ~
Negative request form.
ここで たばこを すわないでください。
Koko de tabako o suwanaide kudasai.
Please don't smoke here.
〜てもいいです — It's okay to ~
Giving permission.
ここに すわってもいいです。
Koko ni suwatte mo ii desu.
You may sit here.
〜てはいけません — Must not ~
Prohibition.
ここで たべてはいけません。
Koko de tabete wa ikemasen.
You must not eat here.
〜が すきです / きらいです — Like / Dislike ~
Express preferences. Use が not を.
わたしは すしが すきです。
Watashi wa sushi ga suki desu.
I like sushi.
〜が できます — Can do ~
Express ability.
にほんごが すこし できます。
Nihongo ga sukoshi dekimasu.
I can speak a little Japanese.
Verb て-form + ください — Please do ~
Polite request.
ゆっくり はなしてください。
Yukkuri hanashite kudasai.
Please speak slowly.

🎯 Adjective Conjugation

い-adjectives — Conjugation Rules
い-adj: present (+い), negative (-くない), past (-かった), past negative (-くなかった)
たかい → たかくない → たかかった → たかくなかった
takai → takaku nai → takakatta → takaku nakatta
expensive → not expensive → was expensive → wasn't expensive
な-adjectives — Conjugation Rules
な-adj: present (+です), negative (+じゃないです), past (+でした), negative past (+じゃなかったです)
しずかです → しずかじゃないです → しずかでした
shizuka desu → shizuka ja nai desu → shizuka deshita
quiet → not quiet → was quiet

🔢 Counters (助数詞)

Common N5 Counters
Japanese uses different counters for different types of objects.
〜まい (flat things): いちまい、にまい | 〜ほん (long things): いっぽん | 〜ひき (small animals): いっぴき | 〜だい (machines): いちだい | 〜にん (people): ひとり、ふたり、さんにん
Sheets/flat: 1 sheet, 2 sheets | Long: 1 (bottle/pen) | Small animals | Machines | People: 1, 2, 3 people

🗣️ Speaking & Pronunciation Guide

🎯 The #1 Speaking Secret

Japanese pronunciation is very consistent — unlike English! Once you know the 5 vowel sounds, you can pronounce almost everything correctly. The vowels never change: あ(a), い(i), う(u), え(e), お(o).

🔊 Vowel Sounds (母音)

The 5 Japanese Vowels
These are PURE vowels — held steady, no diphthongs. Short and crisp.
あ = "ah" (father) | い = "ee" (feet, short) | う = "oo" (food, no lip rounding) | え = "eh" (bed) | お = "oh" (boat, short)

⚠️ Tricky Sounds

つ (tsu) — No English equivalent
Like the "ts" in "bits" but as a syllable start. Practice: "tsunami."
ら り る れ ろ (R sounds)
Japanese "r" is NOT English R or Spanish R. Tap your tongue lightly behind front teeth — between English L and D. Practice: "ladder" said quickly.
ふ (fu) — Soft F
Blow air through barely parted lips. Not a strong "f" — more like "hu" with f.
Long Vowels (長音)
Holding a vowel twice as long changes meaning! おじさん (uncle) vs おじいさん (grandfather). In katakana marked with ー.
ここ (here) vs こうこう (high school)
Double Consonants (促音) っ
Small っ means pause briefly before the next consonant. Like holding your breath for a beat.
きて (please come) vs きって (stamp) — kite vs kitte
Pitch Accent (ピッチアクセント)
Japanese uses pitch (high/low) not stress accent. はし can mean chopsticks (ha↑shi↓), bridge (ha↓shi↑), or edge. Context usually clarifies. Don't stress over this for N5!

📅 3-Month N5 Study Plan

⏱️ Recommended Daily Study Time

30–60 minutes per day is ideal. Consistency matters more than long sessions. Use a spaced repetition system (Anki) for vocabulary and kanji every day.

🗓️ Month 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1-4)

  • Week 1: Learn all 46 Hiragana. Write each 10×/day. Use apps like Dr. Moku or Duolingo.
  • Week 2: Master Hiragana reading. Start 46 Katakana. Learn 50 basic vocabulary words.
  • Week 3: Complete Katakana. Start reading simple texts in kana. Learn 50 more vocab words.
  • Week 4: Review kana. Intro to grammar: は、です、particle basics. First 20 N5 kanji (numbers + time).

🗓️ Month 2 — Core Building (Weeks 5-8)

  • Week 5: Grammar: verb present/past forms, key particles (を、に、で). 20 new kanji.
  • Week 6: Grammar: adjectives (い/な types), あります/います. 20 new kanji. 100 more vocab.
  • Week 7: Grammar: て-form, requests, permission/prohibition. 20 more kanji.
  • Week 8: Grammar: negative forms, questions, expressing wants. 23 final N5 kanji. 100 vocab.

🗓️ Month 3 — Practice & Polish (Weeks 9-12)

  • Week 9: Review all grammar. Read N5-level texts. Take first mock test to identify weak areas.
  • Week 10: JLPT N5 listening practice. Shadowing exercises. Fill vocabulary gaps.
  • Week 11: Full practice tests under timed conditions (2× this week).
  • Week 12: Light review, rest, confidence building. You're ready! 🎌

🔗 Resources & Test Info

📝 About the JLPT N5 Test

Test Structure
Section 1: Language Knowledge (Vocabulary + Kanji) — 25 min | Section 2: Language Knowledge (Grammar) + Reading — 50 min | Section 3: Listening — 30 min | Total: ~105 minutes
Scoring
Total 180 points. Passing score: 80 points (44%). Each section has a minimum required score. Vocabulary/Grammar/Reading sections add to ~120 pts; Listening ~60 pts.
Registration
JLPT is held twice yearly: July and December. Register at jlpt.jp. Test available in 85+ countries. Results in February (July test) and March (December test).

🎯 Final Exam Tips

✅ Test Day Strategy

• Vocabulary section: Read all options before choosing. Eliminate wrong kanji readings first.
• Grammar section: Pay attention to particles — they're frequently tested.
• Reading section: Skim first, then answer questions. Look for key words.
• Listening section: Listen for the QUESTION being asked, not just content. Take brief notes.
• Timing: Don't spend too long on any one question. Move on and return.
• Practice with official JLPT sample tests available at jlpt.jp!
